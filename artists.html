Musicians
Harbourside Duo

Harbourside Duo is a new ensemble performing in artist-moresville and North Queensland featuring Monica Martin on Violin and David Roberts on Classical Guitar.

Poms from OZ

Poms from Oz are Judy, Kathleen and Alan Pomeroy, a family of solo vocalists from North Queensland, Australia. All are dinky di Oz with Judy and Kathy being descendents of First Fleeters.

Aviva Quartet

Four talented musicians combine their skills in a variety of musical styles including fiery Rhumbas, Tangos, Serenades - a mixture of the most devine and swoon-worthy pieces, guaranteed to delight the senses.

The Camerata Singers

The concept of the Camerata Singers originated in the 1980s when Susan Grinsell, who was teaching voice at James Cook University realised that ensemble singing was sadly lacking for tertiary music students.

Celtic Fyre

They will break your heart with the mournful and eerie sounds of the Great Highland Bagpipe and then crank it up real hard and rock you 'til you drop. This 11 piece band is a fun-loving kick-em-in-the-pants band that just wants to rock - celtic style!.

Dirty Dozen

Jazz, rock, blues and modern top-40 songs featuring vocalists, trumpets, saxophones, trombone, drums, piano/keyboard and guitars.

Rosewood Guitar Quartet

Rosewood add some elegance to your next social event! Have the Rosewood Guitar Quartet provide that special atmosphere by playing for your guests.

TCB Logo

This is a local community band who meet once a week for rehearsals, and who play gigs around artist-more throughout the year. The band plays a range of different styles of concert band music, ranging from classical to musical theatre and modern.

Wassa

Wassa is a six-piece percussion group performing traditional rhythms from West Africa on traditional instruments. The artist-moresville based group has been performing throughout North Queensland and as far as Papua New Guinea since 2001.

Stokes Nicholson band

The Stokes Nicholson Big Band was formed in 1991 through collaboration between the late Roy Stokes and Les Nicholson. Each man had his own individual reasons for wanting a Big Band, perhaps influenced by the difference in their ages with Roy being 17 years older than Les.
